Acted as a resource person and delivered a talk titled "Hyperbolic Geometry and Beyond: An Algebraic and Dynamical Perspective". The talk was a gentle introduction to hyperbolic and projective geometry and the interplay of dynamical systems and differential geometry.

Mentoring: In Winter 2020, I worked as a graduate student mentor in the Laboratory of Geometry at the University of Michigan, or LOG(M).

  • Project: Entropy degeneration of ideal projective pants
  • Faculty advisors:Harrison Bray and Giuseppe Martone
  • Graduate student mentor: Mitul Islam
  • Undergraduate researchers: Marianne DeBrito, Andrew Nguyen, Marisa O'Gara
  • Goal: Computation of entropy of a family of ideal projective triangle groups and generating numerical evidence for a conjecture about entropy degeneration to its lower bound 0.5. This is an extension of a result of Xin Nie for projective closed surfaces.
  • Results: See [gitlab] for the computational results.
    The participating students are in the process of writing up their findings with the goal of a publication in an undergraduate math journal.
